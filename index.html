Jose Garcia

A resourceful, proactive environmental professional with several years of experience in the environmental sustainability and management fields. Skilled in stakeholder relations and communication for government, corporate and industrial settings. Strong leadership skills involving managing, developing and motivating teams to achieve their objectives. Analytical and organised approach to the development and implementation of projects that increase efficiency and reduce operating costs whilst fostering a strong team environment. Demonstrated expertise in leading complex environmental programs. Jose is an Operations Supervisor at the Regional Compost Facility, and has been an Environmental Scientist and Project Manager with an academic background in environmental science and engineering. He has 15 years of experience in the environmental sustainability field, managing organics recycling and biosolids composting operations. As a consultant he helped clients manage environmental liabilities in adherence with municipal, regional, provincial and federal regulatory requirements. He has also worked with industry associations to increase environmental awareness and improve industry practices. Over his career, Jose has managed and successfully delivered over 100 projects for a wide range of clients including private individuals, commercial enterprises, industrial manufacturers, insurance and lending institutions, First Nation communities, municipal and regional governments and utilities (i.e., oil and gas). Jose worked on the Sustainable Winegrowing British Columbia program from 2008 to 2011, and was the lead author of Chapter 5 Water and Wastewater in the vineyard sustainability guidebook and assessment.
